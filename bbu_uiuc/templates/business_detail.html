The idea for the platform began inside a student GroupMe called “The BlackSideIllini” (created by Starr & Daniel) where students shared resources, asked questions, and helped each other find services around campus.

When someone asked for a barber, hairstylist, photographer, or other service, the answers often came quickly—but they were buried within thousands of messages. For business owners to secure the opportunity, they had to see the message immediately and jump in before the conversation moved on.

But many of those business owners were students themselves

Ty, the founder of the platform, saw the same pattern happen over and over again. Great businesses existed within the community, but visibility depended on timing, connections, and luck.

In February 2025, Ty decided to build a solution. He released a video on Instagram debuting the first version of BBUIUC, a simple directory designed to make these businesses easier to discover.

The first version was built by writing HTML and JavaScript directly into a content block on Hipolink. What started as a small experiment quickly spread through the community, with the video reaching over 5,000 views as students shared it and businesses began joining the directory.

What began as a response to a problem inside a student group chat has grown into a platform focused on one goal:

Making it easier for students to discover and support businesses within their community.
